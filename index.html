ABOUT ME

I love creating stop-animation films

A highly skilled creator of stop motion animation, I am a graduate of the Animation Program at Howard Country Public Schools Applied Research Lab and am currently enrolled in Film program at Howard Community College. I am a leading contributor and editor of an international group of animators who produce collaborative film projects. I was the third place winner in the Howard Country Public Schools Film Festival in 2021. I am a consumer and connoisseur of all things animated.

Andrew Coffee


In my personal work, I produce stop-motion animated films. Three common themes in my work are creative set designs, meticulous attention to detail and use of outside source materials. My films use clever set designs as I strive to create scenes that are pleasing to the viewer. I aim to create sets and backgrounds that are as complete as possible without causing distraction from the main action or for the viewing audience. I commonly use ordinary objects like towels, tin foil, plastic wrap, and bed sheets in building these sets. I collect small props to use in scenes and also utilize a 3D printer to fabricate objects I do not already have. In my videos, I pay close attention to details like layout and perspective that make the scenes more complete or seem larger than they actually are. In the filming and editing process, I exercise great patience in creating challenging frame by frame videos. Whether filming live-action shorts or stop-motion editing, I am very focused on the small details to produce as clean of a product as possible. In choosing the content for my films, I frequently used outside source materials such as television shows and movies. In creating my films, I sometimes directly use these materials and editing into my project. Other times, I either reference these materials or use them as inspiration as a pay homage to the original source. Throughout my films, I try to infuse my sense of humor and creativity to pass along to the audience.
